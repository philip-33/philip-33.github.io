Wordpress Ecommerce

Lecture: Wordpress Ecommerce Core Concepts

Understanding your market


Choosing a product to sell
There are a lot of considerations to make before making an online store: Areas of interest, types of products, defining an audience
The primary distinction for products are virtual and physical. Each has their own set of logistics and considerations for your site.
Defining your audience
Figuring out your customer base is done by modeling potential customers (personas)
Using a single persona is best for sites at the beginning, and will drive site decisions in the future as more details are added
Organizing your products
Bundling products help users understand it better
An alternative to bundles is to have a single, customizable product or a subscription service
Offering pre-sales to validate your product
Product validation is a way to limit risk when starting a new venture.
Kickstarter, selling a single product, or pre-orders are good ways to gauge interest in your product

Marketing your site


Using marketing to increase site traffic
Increasing traffic requires time, money, or both
SEO is a critical component of this, and ties into social media
Understanding customer lifetime value (CLV)
CLV is a measure of profit over the lifetime of a customer. This value needs to be greater than the cost of acquiring the customer
Bundles, newsletters, and upselling are examples of known methods of increasing CLV
Increasing CLV with repeat customers
Consumable products result in continual sales, otherwise social media is a subtle reminder
Autoresponders can be used to reach out to customers that have made purchases for a category of product (blueberry flavored stuff), or have not engaged with your site for an extended period
Increasing your average order value
By creatively bundling expensive products (yoga mat + bag + cleaner), more expensive items seem reasonable
Cross-selling and upselling are other ways to increase order value, and are often configurable from a dashboard
Free shipping at is a good tactic

Selling Products


Getting paid
Payment taken from customer by payment gateway
Traditional gateway uses merchant account and CC payment. Modern gateway does not require merchant account
Only capture a verified charge when the product ships
Examining your needs
Multiple ways to take payments (stripe, paypal, amazon, CC gateway)
Analyze risk before deciding on a method
Checking out with PayPal
PP can integrate with some sites, hiding it’s existence until the checkout process goes to PayPal
Excellent for just getting started
Checking out with Stripe
This functions just like PP, except “Credit Card” is selected as default payment method.
After entering CC info, payment is authorized on the same page, and the payment is complete.

Shipping, Orders, Taxes


Calculating shipping costs
Flat Rate: single, easy cost to calculate shipping ($5 + $2 per item)
Estimates require accounts with shippers and your site needs to communicate through their apis
Anything beyond flat rate takes time and is not recommended to start
Managing orders
Wordpress stores hopefully include user roles for customers/managers by default
With physical product, broken orders are inevitable. For refunds, not all gateways can handle this from the API. Investigate refund process first!
Managing inventory and listings across platforms
Brand reach increases with additional sites to list products
Different platforms have different focuses (sales, inventory, accounting, shipping)
Understanding taxes
Charged based on nexus (physical presence), collected once
Some payment gateways simplify payments and do not take billing details
Reach out to a tax professional
Collecting EU value-added tax (VAT)
VAT is europe’s version of sales tax, rolled into the cost of each item
A good service will automatically calculate these for you, including taking into account location based on self-report and IP

Securing your site


Understanding the basics of SSL
More security == more sales
SSL encrypts all data between your website and customers. Mandatory for payments, but optional for browsing and cart management.
Protecting sensitive data
HTTPS should be forced for the entire site whenever possible
Additionally, all logins should be over https

Using Wordpress for Ecommerce


Using native Wordpress platforms
WP is open source, so many features are provided for free and can be freely replaced as necessary
More advanced platforms require payment to access all features or are payment based because of specific functionality
Implementing third-party platforms
When picking a 3rd party platform, be aware of features, limitations, costs associated with it. The wrong one will significantly hamper your efforts
Knowing how your site will work and what it needs to function is critical here.

Testing and Optimization


Testing your site
Test the cart and checkout process. It is the most critical. Second is the shipping
Final test is for payment gateways, then the rest of your site (membership, pricing, newsletters, services).
Small errors now = major problems later
Optimizing your site
When optimizing for ecommerce, data analytics by yoast is a solution with free and paid components
Good user research can identify problems on your site
