This site was made for Rio Wallerâ€™s CIT 82 Web Technologies class at Fresno City College. The content here reflects work completed during the class, organized into categories that we picked as relevant to learning web development skills. Personally, these categories represent topics that I saw as important for my goal of not just becoming a developer but a professional one. This means not just being able to program, but able to see the bigger picture of how components fit together and whether a particular development or design goal is worthwhile, and to find a better solution that is more than just a hack if necessary.

Even with some previous experience, this set of topics stands on its own as the beginning of the knowledge necessary to be an effective developer. These selections are intended to be directly related to the tools and skills that, to my understanding, a developer would be expected to implicitly understand about the internet, their tools, and how they interact. While not exhaustive, this information would allow someone who is interested but not necessarily familiar with all the topics to begin development on a modern, responsive website as well as identify the next steps they would need to take, depending on their goals.
