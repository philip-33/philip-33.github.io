UI/UX

Based on the first video, I got that neither UI or UX are explicitly developer positions, and wouldn't necessarily be done by a developer in a large enough group. Obviously, they both require a developer to implement. Just browsing through the topics at the User Experience StackExchange give a good indication of a UX designer's concerns. When working on client websites, this is something that takes up a good chunk of my time and is something that I stress is important to my clients.

While design isn't something I'm very good at, I've had to get my hands dirty occasionally and based on the things I've learned and seen is one primary metric for success: if a user is able to do/find exactly what they want on your app/site/whatever without ever having to hunt for a button or even think about where to go to find it, the design is well done. I've heard this referred to as the design or interface "disappearing" for the user.
